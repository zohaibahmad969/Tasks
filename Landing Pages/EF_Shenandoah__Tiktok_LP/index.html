com·mu·ni·ty /k(ə)'mju.nə.ti/ noun

  1. a feeling of fellowship with others, as a result of sharing common living space, characteristics, interests, and goals.

ACRE /ˈākər/ (noun)

  1. A unit of land area equal to 4,840 square yards.

  2. The Albright Creative Research Experience (ACRE), a student-led research experience over the summer (10 weeks) or January term (3 weeks), during which students and faculty learn from each other as they explore topics of interest from a vast array of disciplines.

Ex: “Did you hear about Jamie’s research on the intersection of virtual reality and modern composition? They did it as an interim ACRE project!”

4th Hour of Quality /fôrTH ˈou(ə)r ˈkwälədē/ (noun)

  1. The time between the 3rd hour and the 5th hour.

  2. A fourth credit that students earn for each class by participating in included labs or weekly lectures, OR that can be earned through experiential learning, attending events, or collaborating with faculty.

Ex: “I went to a guest lecture series last night for Fourth-hour of quality credit, and it changed my life, no cap! I might have found my future career right there!”

Pillar r (Pillars, pl) /ˈpilər/ (noun)

  1. A tall, vertical structure or stone, wood, or metal, used as a support for a building.

  2. The foundation of Allbright’s core values that reflect our unique culture and sense of campus community that set the stage for the Albright student experience.

Ex: “We talk about integrity, curiosity, connection, and resilience a lot, and no wonder—everything we do is built on those concepts as the Albright pillars.”
